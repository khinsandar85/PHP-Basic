Yangon (formerly known as Rangoon) is the largest city in Myanmar (formerly Burma). A mix of
British colonial architecture, modern high-rises and gilded Buddhist pagodas define its skyline. Its
famed Shwedagon Paya, a huge, shimmering pagoda complex, draws thousands pilgrims annually.
The city's other notable religious sites include the Botataung and Su le pagodas, both the housing
Buddhist relics.
